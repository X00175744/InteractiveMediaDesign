Project

Second Year

In 2nd year, I was tasked with creating a mock e-retail platform. I was assigned to a team with two others, Jason Fung and Alan Byrne. Both talented programmers. Together, we created GTU, Gamer Time Universal, a platform that showcased new game titles for competitive prices. Of course, we weren't actually selling anything but it had all the functionality to make you think it did. The project was built in Python, using the Django framework.

We split up the tasks amongst ourselves. By the end of the project, I had contributed the following functionalities: Display Products Add Products Categories Product Detail Search Products Order Log Functionality Product Review As well as UI styling Of course, we all helped each other out when we needed to. It wasn't as if we worked in isolated silence, after all. In the end, we created a website that had full CRUD functionality for users, a home page with featured products, a "massive savings" tab that highlighted games whose price had fallen, could filter games based on both price and genre, could display information on each game, allow you to add a product to your basket (only if you were logged in), check out and pay using Stripe, check previous orders using order logs, leave reviews with ratings and edit those reviews later. It was an in-depth project that took a lot of hard work and earned us an A.

Experience

I worked as a Patient Services Administrator in the Beacon Hospital for two years. My job was to manage patient appointments, register patients and offer guidance where necessary. Through this job, I interacted daily with a live hospital system and database, as well as developed my people skills by talking to patients for registration, reassuring those who were nervous and explaining details of procedures, as well as communicating with my team and other teams we worked with. For instance, in radiology, not only do you need to communicate with others working directly with you, but also with the radiology team performing the scans and procedures, who need you to correctly prepare patients for procedures with consent forms and wristbands.

I got a wide range of experience working in the Beacon, as I moved from department to department. All in all, I worked in Day Unit, Radiology, Emergency Department, Urology and Endoscopy. Each department handles patients and patient information differently, depending on the nature of the procedures. From this position, I learned to be adaptable and react quickly. I also developed my people skills, as well as broadening my experience with computer systems.

As part of my third year, I interned in OpenJaw Technologies for nine months beginning in February 2023. OpenJaw Technologies specialises in travel technology. They develop software that allows airlines to manage and display accommodation information from suppliers, such as Expedia. While I was there I got a lot of valuable experience working in the tech industry. I got well acquainted with Java, the language of choice for OpenJaw. Not only did I learn about how Java is used now, but also how it developed. I worked primarily on a content loader for a supplier that was in need of updating. While some files were written in older Java, this content loader was old enough that some files were written in Groovy, an old Java offshoot language that got reintegrated into Java 8. When working with this, and figuring out what Groovy was and how to read it, I got the chance to ask why Groovy was good at the time, and why it is no longer being used. Why it was made obselete and what modern languages do better.

I got much valuable experience working with OpenJaw, but most importantly to me, I got to see what a tech sector position is like and what that means for me and my career going forward.

Interests

I've had an interest in art from a young age. My grandmother instilled this in me. Given that she enjoyed painting herself, it served as a way for us to connect. I carried it through most of my life, taking Art in secondary school, where I learned more about it as a subject. I learned what I do and don't like, namely, art theory. I found that, while I enjoyed the process of drawing, I had little interest for the minute details and terminology. I valued the process of creating more than the history of the subject.

I began to explore the idea of art in a digital medium, which, in turn, led to my interest in graphic design. I taught myself the ins and outs of drawing software as well as editing software, and had fun messing around with photos or drawing on the computer. This helped to nurture my interest in computing as a whole, I believe.

For as long as I can remember, gaming has been an interest of mine. When I was younger, my brothers introduced me to it with the Playstation 2. It was a hobby we shared and spent much time pursuing. As I grew older, I found friends who shared this hobby and, through them, I found new ways to enjoy gaming. I discovered strategy games, which quickly became one of my favourite genres. I discovered the social aspects of online play, and that gaming can be used a means to keep in contact. Even when abroad, we could always hop on for a quick game of Civilisation V or Project Zomboid. In that way, gaming has been a great comfort to me through my life, and has been a way for me to better appreciate the people around me. It's a hobby I don't intend to drop anytime soon.

Compared to the previous two, this hobby is a more recent one. I discovered a joy for Dungeons and Dragons through a friend of mine a few years ago, and found I had a knack for being the Dungeon Master, the one to lead each game. I particularly enjoyed writing new storylines for my players to interact with, excited to see how they would react and what they would do or say.

From this came a new hobby, writing. I found that one of my favourite parts of playing DnD, other than actually playing, was writing worlds for players to explore. It occurred to me that I could do so beyond the confines of a DnD game. I could try making a world to base stories in. I'd read books from an early age, and had recently finished the Dune series when I decided to lay the groundwork for a setting to place my first story in. I found I enjoyed the process of writing, thinking through the consequences of different story elements and discussing it with others. All in all, it's a hobby I hope to explore more in the future.
